Margherita pizza


Description

Margherita pizza is a classic Italian pizza that is typically made with a thin crust, tomato sauce, fresh mozzarella cheese, basil, and olive oil. The crust is usually baked until it is crispy and golden brown, and the sauce is made from fresh tomatoes that are seasoned with garlic and other herbs. The mozzarella cheese is placed on top of the sauce, and the pizza is finished with a sprinkle of fresh basil and a drizzle of olive oil. The result is a delicious and simple pizza that is loved by many for its fresh and bright flavors.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 pound pizza dough
  • 1/4 cup tomato sauce
  • 4 ounces shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 2 ounces sliced pepperoni
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • Flour, for dusting

Steps

  1. Preheat your oven to 450 degrees F.
  2. Lightly dust a work surface with flour and roll out the pizza dough to desired thickness.
  3. Transfer the dough to a baking sheet and brush with olive oil.
  4. Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese over the tomato sauce.
  6. Top with sliced pepperoni.
  7. Bake for 12-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Remove from oven and let cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.
